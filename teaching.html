Courses taught at the Department of Mechanical Engineering, KFUPM.

Courses

Current & recent courses

ME 201
Dynamics

Kinematics and kinetics of particles and rigid bodies. Newton's laws, work-energy methods, impulse-momentum, and an introduction to vibrations.

Undergraduate · 3 credit hours
ME 482
Mechanical Vibrations

Free and forced vibrations of single and multi-degree-of-freedom systems. Damping, resonance, frequency response, and an introduction to continuous systems.

Undergraduate · 3 credit hours
ME 401
System Dynamics and Control

Modeling of dynamic systems — mechanical, electrical, and electromechanical. Transfer functions, state-space representation, feedback control design, stability analysis, and frequency response methods.

Undergraduate · 3 credit hours
ME 553
Advanced Vibrations

Review of single degree of freedom oscillator: formulation using generalized stiffness, inertia and damping. Damping mechanisms: viscous, friction, and complex. Response to transient and general excitations. Multiple degrees of freedom systems: formulation and methods of solution. Direct stiffness, influence coefficients and variational approaches. Eigenvalue analysis. Vibration of continuous systems. Approximation methods of continuous systems. Modal reduction technique
