About

What we work on

The iMSS Group is a newly established research group in the Department of Mechanical Engineering at KFUPM, also affiliated with the Interdisciplinary Research Center for Intelligent Manufacturing and Robotics.

Our interest is in programmable piezoelectric metamaterials — structures whose wave-propagation behavior can be tuned digitally by adjusting electrical circuits connected to each unit cell. This opens up possibilities for reconfigurable wave devices, vibration control systems, and energy harvesting applications.

We are at an early stage and actively building the group. If the research sounds interesting to you, we would be glad to hear from you.

Research

Current directions

01
Programmable Wave Control

Using graded piezoelectric arrays with digitally tuned shunt circuits to trap, compress, and steer elastic waves along a waveguide.

02
Nonreciprocal Propagation

Spatiotemporal modulation of electrical impedance to break wave reciprocity — enabling elastic waves to travel preferentially in one direction.

03
Energy Harvesting

Graded metastructures that simultaneously attenuate vibrations and harvest the trapped energy through the direct piezoelectric effect.

04
Smart Structures

Digitally programmable resonant elastic metamaterials using synthetic impedance circuits that allow rapid reconfiguration without hardware changes.

Dispersion in a piezoelectric metamaterial

The dispersion relation describes how elastic waves propagate through a periodic metamaterial. When piezoelectric unit cells are shunted to inductive circuits, a band gap opens near the shunt resonant frequency — a range of frequencies where wave propagation is forbidden.

Adjust the sliders to see how the band gap responds to changes in shunt frequency, electromechanical coupling strength, and damping.
