01
Dispersion relation of a piezoelectric metamaterial
Band gap formation · Electromechanical coupling · Damping effects
+

The dispersion relation describes how elastic waves propagate through a periodic piezoelectric beam. When each unit cell is shunted to an inductive circuit, a band gap opens near the shunt resonant frequency \(\omega_t\) — a frequency range where no propagating wave solutions exist.

The gap spans \([\omega_t,\; \omega_t\sqrt{1+\alpha}]\), where \(\alpha = \theta^2 / (EI \cdot C_p)\) is the dimensionless electromechanical coupling. Higher \(\alpha\) → wider gap.

Increasing the damping ratio \(\zeta\) softens the gap edges — the curves fade into the gap rather than cutting off sharply. At \(\zeta = 0\) you recover the undamped theory.

02
Tip transmissibility of a graded piezoelectric metastructure
Rainbow trapping · Band-gap widening · Energy harvesting
+

A cantilever beam with \(Q = 25\) piezoelectric unit cells shunted to resistive-inductive circuits. In the uniform case all shunts are tuned to the same frequency \(\omega_t\). In the graded case the resonant frequency varies along the beam:

\[\omega_{t,k} = \omega_t + \delta - 2\delta\!\left(\frac{k}{Q-1}\right)^{\!p}\]

Grading widens the attenuation band. The power \(p\) controls the spatial profile — try \(p = 0\) (uniform), \(p = 1\) (linear), \(p = 2\) (quadratic), and \(p = 0.5\) (fractional) to compare.

Vibration is attenuated where \(|\text{TR}| \leq 0.1\) (dashed gold line).
